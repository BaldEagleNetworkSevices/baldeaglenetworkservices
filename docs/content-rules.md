# Content Rules Lock

## Required Message Priorities

- Lead with recovery outcomes before generic support language.
- Keep recovery validation, restore testing, backup scope, and recovery dependencies explicit.
- Keep security messaging tied to recovery speed and containment.

## Disallowed Drift

- Do not position the site as a generic outsourced technology catalog.
- Do not position Microsoft 365 as the primary narrative.
- Do not position general ticket-queue support as the front-door offer.
- Do not use vague resource-language for proof pages (for example: "resources", "insights", "core pages").

## CTA Rules

- Primary CTA across core pages: Recovery Assessment.
- Case study and proof sections must include a clear path back to Recovery Assessment.
- Keep header action area to one primary CTA on desktop.

## Navigation and IA Rules

- Keep the case study visible as "Case Study" in primary navigation.
- Do not duplicate the case-study path as competing secondary actions in the desktop header.
- Keep footer headings business-oriented and recovery-focused.

## Routing and SEO Rules

- Inactive/parked pages must stay in `waiting_pages/`.
- Inactive pages must not be linked from active navigation, footer, or active section blocks.
- Inactive pages must not be included in page catalog, page definitions, sitemap, or indexable routes unless intentionally restored.
